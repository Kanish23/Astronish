Key to Wonder, Telescopes!

Since the beginning, humanity has only been able to understand our universe through what they see. The night sky back then used to be fully exposed without the existence of light pollution. So, although there was a lot to see, some wanted to see even closer. In this section, we are going to understand the significance of telescopes in the future of astronomy.

Telescope History

The history of the telescope begins in the early 17th century when Dutch spectacle makers invented a device that could magnify distant objects. Galileo Galilei significantly improved upon the design and was the first to use it for astronomical observations in 1609. His discoveries—such as the moons of Jupiter and the phases of Venus—revolutionized our understanding of the universe. Over the centuries, telescopes evolved from simple refractors to powerful space-based observatories like the Hubble Space Telescope, drastically expanding our view of the cosmos.
